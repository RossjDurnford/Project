What made the Greeks Different?


The Greeks were in fact very interesting for many reasons, one reason being the Olympic Games originated from Ancient Greece. However Greece was the only country involved at this time but the Olympics have been modernised to include the whole world.


The Greeks also had amazing architectural skills. Some Greek buildings whilst ruins are still around today due to the level of skill and perserverance of the Greek people. These bulildings mostly are ruins however they have stood for thousands of years. A couple of examples of their incredible perseverance include the Parthenon and the Acropolis.


Democracy is a Greek concept (who knew?). The Greeks used to have elections and elect their own leaders through a democratic process not to disimillar from what is used in today's modern society. They also had a judicial system, the trials were mainly disputes between people and no lawyers would have been present. The punishments would usually fit the crime and the Greeks tried to avoid capital punishment as a deterrent, where instead people would be exiled from their city and forced to find an alternative means of living.




According to experts, the Parthenon was used as a treasury in Greece after construction in the 5th century B.C. It was also considered the center of religious life in Athens. Still standing today it was a display of power, wealth and elevated culture situated in Athens.
